Teaching

Teaching Statement

Teaching is an important component of my graduate life. I enjoy communicating with my students, sharing knowledge, helping them with questions or walking them through sections that they struggle with. From my own experience, learning statistics, or STEM in general, is an interesting but challenging task which requires practice and patience.
In my labs and office hours, in-person or virtual, I always encourage interation. Sometimes when you are really confused, it might be hard to articulate questions. In each of my lab/discussion sessions, I try to make sure that students feel comfortable asking questions or adding comments, including the ones that are not well formulated or blurry.

Teaching has been an important component of my life throughout graduate school. I have served as a teaching assistant or instructor to courses from undergraduate-level to graduate-level, ranging from accelerated proof-based classes for math or stats majors, general data science courses for non-STEM majors, to management decision courses for business and engineering graduates.
